MAUT KI GHATI — Episode 1: "The Valley Calls"
Written for Zupee Studio

═══════════════════════════════════════════════

COLD OPEN

EXT. MAUT KI GHATI — MOUNTAIN ROAD — DAWN

A battered jeep climbs a narrow road carved into the cliff face.
The valley below is still dark, wrapped in cold mist.

TITLE CARD: "Maut Ki Ghati. Population: 847. Disappearances in the last decade: 23."

INT. JEEP — CONTINUOUS

PRIYA SHARMA (32) drives, a worn notebook on the passenger seat.
Her phone shows no signal. She doesn't seem concerned.

PRIYA
(to herself)
Twenty-three people. And nobody asks why.

She photographs the valley through the windscreen.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════

ACT ONE

EXT. MAUT KI GHATI — VILLAGE SQUARE — MORNING

The village is quiet. Too quiet for 8 AM.
An old man sweeps a doorstep and stops when he sees Priya.

OLD MAN
(in Hindi, subtitled)
Journalists don't come here.

PRIYA
(in Hindi, subtitled)
This one does. I'm looking for Arun Dixit. I was told he lives here.

OLD MAN
Arun Dixit left three months ago.

PRIYA
Left or disappeared?

The old man stares at her. Then goes inside without another word.

Priya writes in her notebook: "Village knows more than they say."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════

INT. MAUT KI GHATI POLICE STATION — DAY

A small room. A single desk. One officer.

VIKRAM RATHORE (38) looks up from a file. His face is unreadable —
the kind of face that has practised being unreadable for years.

VIKRAM
You're the journalist from Delhi.

PRIYA
Word travels fast in a valley with no phone signal.

VIKRAM
(not smiling)
What do you want?

PRIYA
I want to know about the twenty-three missing persons cases
in this valley over the last decade. I want to know why
none of them were escalated beyond district level.
I want to know who is making that decision.

A long pause. Vikram closes the file on his desk.

VIKRAM
People move. They go to the city. They find work.

PRIYA
They leave without their families? Without their savings?
Without their phones?

VIKRAM
(flatly)
I don't know what you've heard. But I suggest you
be careful about what questions you ask here.
Some answers come with consequences.

PRIYA
(meeting his gaze)
Then they're the right questions.

She leaves. Vikram watches her go, then picks up his phone
and dials a number he doesn't say out loud.

VIKRAM
(quietly)
She's here.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════

INT. PRIYA'S RENTED ROOM — NIGHT

Priya spreads files across the bed. Photographs. Missing persons reports.
A map of the valley with red pins marking where each person was last seen.

All the pins cluster near the old mill on the eastern ridge.

PRIYA
(photographing the map)
They're all within two kilometres of the same point.

She pulls out a yellowed newspaper clipping.
Headline: "VALLEY MILL CLOSES — WORKERS PROMISE INVESTIGATION."
The date is 1987. Thirty-seven years ago.

PRIYA (CONT'D)
What were you investigating?

She flips the clipping. On the back, in faded pencil,
her mentor's handwriting: "Follow the serpent."

She stares at it for a long time.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════

EXT. OLD MILL — RIDGE — NIGHT

Priya approaches the abandoned mill with a torch.
The gates are padlocked — but the padlock is new.

PRIYA
(whispering into recorder)
Eastern ridge mill. Gates relocked recently.
Someone is still using this facility.

She finds a gap in the fence and slips through.

INT. MILL — CONTINUOUS

Dust. Rusted machinery. But in one corner — recent footprints in the dust.
Equipment covered with tarps. Priya lifts one corner: modern electronics.
Signal boosters. Recording equipment.

PRIYA (CONT'D)
(photographing)
This isn't abandoned. This is active.

A sound behind her. She kills the torch.

Footsteps. The soft creak of the floor.

PRIYA (CONT'D)
(barely audible)
They're here.

She presses herself against the wall and holds her breath.

A shadow passes the window.

SMASH CUT TO BLACK.

TITLE: MAUT KI GHATI

END OF EPISODE ONE
